CIELO Extraction Guide

Calculated for 100g cactus powder with Fumaric Acid — Water: 130ml | Lime: 25g | EA total: ~950ml | Acid: 3.0g

CIELO

Crystals In Ethyl-acetate Lazily Over-the-counter

In this process, mescaline from cactus is precipitated in ethyl acetate as crystals. This TEK is specialized for cactus, simple, and avoids harsh chemicals. With less than an hour of labor, very pure mescaline can be extracted. However, the process needs to be followed closely to ensure good results.

Please grow your own cacti! San Pedro cacti in Peru are being over-harvested in the wild in a non-sustainable way. Never purchase dry cactus powder online. Obtain and grow live seeds, cuttings, or rooted plants.

Safety

  • Ethyl acetate: Flash point 38°F. No open flames! Work in a well ventilated area. Serious eye irritant — safety goggles recommended. Flush with water for 15 min if any gets in your eye.
  • Food safe: Use food grade fumaric or citric acid since some will be in the final product.
  • Dust: Dry cactus powder and lime release fine particles. Dust mask recommended for sensitive lungs.
  • Compatibility: Do not use aluminum vessels with salted (acidic) ethyl acetate — corrosion risk.

Consumables

  • 100 g dry fine cactus powder
  • 130 ml water + 70 ml reserve (max 200 ml)
  • 25 g Ca(OH)₂ (lime)
  • ~950 ml ethyl acetate (sometimes sold as “MEK substitute”)
  • 3.0 g fumaric acid 5.0 g citric acid (anhydrous or monohydrate)
  • Optional: pH testing strips
  • For solvent reclaim: washing soda (Na₂CO₃)
Warning: Avoid combining PC cactus with citric acid. This combination risks not precipitating anything if yield is low (<0.15%).

Equipment

  • Knife, paper bag, dehydrator, food processor, coffee grinder, flour sifter
  • Gram scale (to measure ingredients)
  • Large bowl and spoon (or gloves) for mixing
  • French press (stainless steel, 34 oz / ~1L or larger)
  • Coffee filters, filter basket, and funnel
  • Quart mason glass jars with lids
  • Milligram scale (to measure product)

Recommended Upgrades

  • Grain mill (finer powder)
  • Stand mixer with flat beater
  • Magnetic stirrer

Process Overview

1. Powder ➔ 2. Wet Crumbs ➔ 3. Extract ➔ 4. Crystallize ➔ 5. Collect ➔ 6. Reuse

Grow, harvest, store, dry, and grind cactus into a fine powder. Do not harvest plants under 3 feet tall. Cut the top half for processing, leave the bottom to regrow. For growing collections: cut at 1/3 and 2/3 height, replant the top third, process the middle.

Stressing (recommended)

Store live cuttings in a dark place for at least 3 months (e.g. in a paper bag). Data shows this can increase mescaline content.

  1. Chop whole cacti into ~1/4 inch thick slices
  2. Dry with dehydrator at low temp (~120°F / 50°C). Low temp prevents chlorophyll breakdown which causes tan color in product.
  3. Coarse grind dry slices (food processor)
  4. Fine grind (coffee grinder or grain mill)
  5. Run through flour sifter, regrind anything caught by the sifter

The powder must be uniformly fine and dry. Clouds of fine dust should fly into the air when handled, reminiscent of flour. Store in a sealed container to prevent moisture absorption.

All parts of the cactus can be used. Outer green skin yields more per dry mass, but mescaline is in both. Waxy layers and spines are not detrimental — removal is optional.

Mix cactus powder, lime, and water into “wet crumbs” — wet enough to stick together when squeezed, dry enough to crumble apart when broken.

If dry spots remain, add small amounts from your 70 ml reserve. A stand mixer at low speed is a great upgrade for this step.

Too wet? If the mix becomes a dough or paste, extraction still works but requires a sodium carbonate partial dry step before crystallization (see Appendix in the original TEK).

Pull wet crumbs with ethyl acetate in the french press. Complete all pulls within 45 minutes — the plant matter will congeal after that, reducing solvent recovery.

Pull 1 (larger volume)

Pulls 2–5 (each ~175 ml)

Watch the final trickle closely to avoid water from the crumbs being squeezed into the solvent. Aim for at least 110 ml back from Pull 1 and all added EA back from subsequent pulls. Dispose spent crumbs on compost (when dry) or trash.

Tip: If loose particles appear or filter clogs, the wet crumbs had dry spots. Filter/decant all particles before salting. Next time, mix more thoroughly with enough water.

Add fumaric acid to the extract. The dissolved acid reacts with mescaline freebase to form insoluble monomescaline fumarate crystals. Add citric acid to the extract. The dissolved acid reacts with mescaline freebase to form insoluble monomescaline citrate crystals.

Choose crystallization method:

Slow (no stirrer)

Let acid dissolve by diffusion without stirring. Leave sealed and undisturbed at room temperature for at least 3 days. Crystals can grow large (>2mm).

Fast (magnetic stirrer)

Fumarate typically crystallizes in 30 minutes or less. Crystals will be very small (powdery). Citrate can take up to 2 hours. Crystals will be very small (powdery).

The mescaline salt crystals can look like undissolved acid to beginners. However, all acid granules reliably dissolve. The mescaline salt floats and makes a “snow globe” when shaken. Undissolved acid will not.

Citric: goo instead of crystals? The ethyl acetate contained too much water. See FAQ for recovery options. Fumaric acid is significantly less sensitive to water content.

Collect, rinse, and dry mescaline salt crystals. Do not store filtered salted solvent in metal containers (excess acid can corrode).

Yield depends on the cactus, usually 0.3% to 3% with 0.6%–1.5% being common. Healthy home-grown bridgesii tend to yield more than pachanoi.

Tip: The collection filter can be reused upside-down before salting the next extract. Residual crystals act as seed crystals for faster crystallization next time.

Regenerate solvent for reuse. The green color does not interfere and stays stable. No limit on reuses. Before washing, check for new crystal growth and collect any product.
